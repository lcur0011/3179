Global Methane Emissions

Methane is a greenhouse gas that contributes to global warming. It has a warming power that is more than 80 times stronger than carbon dioxide.

Methane has both natural and anthropological sources. Only human-produced methane is accounted for in the data

As shown on the map, China, India and the United States of America produce the most methane globally, but when we account for population, Botswana, New Zealand and Uruguay stand out as high-emitting countries.

This bar chart shows the top 7 countries, and the composition of their methane contributions.

For Brazil, China, India, Pakistan and the USA, agriculture is responsible for most of methane produced, while for Indonesia and Russia, waste is responsible.

The pie chart shows that agriculture makes up 63.9% of the total methane emissions, followed by waste which makes up 31.7% and other which makes up the remaining 4.4%.
